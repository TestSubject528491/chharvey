Grade Seven Mathematics Curriculum

Updated Feb 1, 2014.

Grade Seven Mathematics is the standard mathematics course taken by students in their second year of secondary education. Students who successfully complete the Grade Seven Standards of Learning should prepare to study Algebra I in eighth grade. Students who need reinforcing of mathematical abilities before Algebra I should prepare to enroll in Grade Eight Mathematics.

Unit Summaries

This section describes in general the units of the Math 7 curriculum. It also provides links to the Unit Overviews, which contain information such as learning objectives and vocabulary for each unit.

Unit 1: Operations on Integers

This unit focuses on performing, and identifying properties of, operations on whole numbers and integers. Students will simplify expressions involving the four basic operations, and will identify the properties that define a mathematical field and apply those properties in the context of the field of Real Numbers.

  1. Lesson 1.1: Integer Addition
  2. Lesson 1.2: Integer Subtraction
  3. Lesson 1.3: Integer Multiplication and Division
  4. Lesson 1.4: Order of Operations
  5. Lesson 1.5: Substitution
  6. Lesson 1.6: Field Properties

Unit 2: Equations & Inequalities

This unit focuses on finding the solutions of linear equations and inequalities with one variable. Students will translate and distinguish between verbal and mathematical equations. Equations will be solved in at most two steps, and inequalities will be solved in at most one step. Students will set up and solve equations to determine the solutions of real-world problems given in verbal form. Students will also graph solution sets to one-variable inequalities on a number line.

  1. Lesson 2.1: Verbal Mathematics
  2. Lesson 2.2: One-Step Equations
  3. Lesson 2.3: Two-Step Equations
  4. Lesson 2.4: Word Problems
  5. Lesson 2.5: Writing and Graphing Inequalities
  6. Lesson 2.6: Solving Inequalities

Unit 3: Rational Numbers & Proportions

In this unit, students' learning will center on proportional reasoning. Students will explore different representations of positive rational numbers, familiarize their placement with respect to each other in a total order, and make connections between magnitude and order. They will also investigate the concepts of ratio and proportion, and use these to solve practical problems involving real-world scenarios.

  1. Lesson 3.1: Powers of Ten
  2. Lesson 3.2: Scientific Notation
  3. Lesson 3.3: Decimals, Percents, and Fractions
  4. Lesson 3.4: Ordering Rational Numbers
  5. Lesson 3.5: Absolute Value
  6. Lesson 3.6: Ratios and Proportions
  7. Lesson 3.7: Similar Polygons

Unit 4: Patterns & Functions

This unit explores patterns of integers and rational numbers and relationships between two variables. Students will analyze and generalize sequences of integers and rational numbers. They will also explore different forms of relations using a variety of representations, and will identify and derive rules for functions.

  1. Lesson 4.1: Sequences
  2. Lesson 4.2: The Coordinate Plane
  3. Lesson 4.3: Finite Relations
  4. Lesson 4.4: Graphing Lines
  5. Lesson 4.5: Writing Rules
  6. Lesson 4.6: Distinguishing Functions

Unit 5: Geometry

  1. Lesson 5.1: Perfect Squares
  2. Lesson 5.2: Rectangular Prisms
  3. Lesson 5.3: Cylinders
  4. Lesson 5.4: Comparing Volume and Surface Area
  5. Lesson 5.5: Sides and Angles of Quadrilaterals
  6. Lesson 5.6: Diagonals of Quadrilaterals
  7. Lesson 5.7: Transformations

Unit 6: Probability & Statistics

  1. Lesson 6.1: Experimental Probability
  2. Lesson 6.2: Theoretical Probability
  3. Lesson 6.3: The Fundamental Counting Principle
  4. Lesson 6.4: Histograms

State of Virginia Standards of Learning

This section lists the Math 7 VA SOLs and includes links to standards-based quizzes for each.

7.1abc Rational Numbers No Calculator

The student will
  1. investigate and describe the concept of negative exponents for powers of ten;
  2. determine scientific notation for numbers greater than zero; and
  3. compare and order fractions, decimals, percents, and numbers written in scientific notation.
  • Version 1

7.1d Perfect Squares No Calculator

The student will determine square roots.
  • Version 1

7.1e Absolute Value

The student will identify and describe absolute value for rational numbers.
  • Version 1

7.2 Sequences

The student will describe and represent arithmetic and geometric sequences, using variable expressions.
  • Version 1

7.3 Integer Operations No Calculator

The student will
  1. model addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of integers; and
  2. add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers.
  • Version 1

7.4 Proportional Reasoning

The student will solve single-step and multistep practical problems, using proportional reasoning.
  • Version 1

7.5 Volume and Surface Area

The student will
  1. describe volume and surface area of cylinders;
  2. solve practical problems involving the volume and surface area of rectangular prisms and cylinders; and
  3. describe how changing one measured attribute of a rectangular prism affects its volume and surface area.
  • Version 1

7.6 Similar Polygons

The student will determine whether plane figures—quadrilaterals and triangles—are similar and write proportions to express the relationships between corresponding sides of similar figures.
  • Version 1

7.7 Quadrilaterals

The student will compare and contrast the following quadrilaterals based on properties: parallelogram, rectangle, square, rhombus, and trapezoid.
  • Version 1

7.8 Transformations

The student, given a polygon in the coordinate plane, will represent transformations (reflections, dilations, rotations, and translations) by graphing in the coordinate plane.
  • Version 1

7.9 Probability

The student will investigate and describe the difference between the experimental probability and theoretical probability of an event.
  • Version 1

7.10 Counting

The student will determine the probability of compound events, using the Fundamental (Basic) Counting Principle.
  • Version 1

7.11 Histograms

The student will, given data for a practical situation, will
  1. construct and analyze histograms; and
  2. compare and contrast histograms with other types of graphs presenting information from the same data set.
  • Version 1

7.12 Relations

The student will represent relationships with tables, graphs, rules, and words.
  • Version 1

7.13a Verbal Mathematics

The student will write verbal expressions as algebraic expressions and sentences as equations and vice versa.
  • Version 1

7.13b Substitution

The student will evaluate algebraic expressions for given replacement values of the variables.

7.14 Linear Equations

The student will
  1. solve one- and two-step linear equations in one variable; and
  2. solve practical problems requiring the solution of one- and two-step linear equations.

7.15 Linear Inequalities

The student will
  1. solve one-step inequalities in one variable; and
  2. graph solutions to inequalities on the number line.

7.16 Field Properties

The student will apply the following properties of operations with real numbers:
  1. the commutative and associative properties for addition and multiplication;
  2. the distributive property
  3. the additive and multiplicative identity properties;
  4. the additive and multiplicative inverse properties; and
  5. the multiplicative property of zero.
