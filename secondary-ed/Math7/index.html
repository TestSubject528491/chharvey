Grade Seven Mathematics Curriculum

Updated Nov 5, 2013.

Grade Seven Mathematics is the standard mathematics course taken by students in their second year of secondary education. Students who successfully complete the Grade Seven Standards of Learning should prepare to study Algebra I in eighth grade. Students who need reinforcing of mathematical abilities before Algebra I should prepare to enroll in Grade Eight Mathematics.

Units in Math 7

Unit 1: Whole Numbers & Integers

This unit focuses on performing, and identifying properties of, operations on whole numbers and integers. Students will simplify expressions involving the four basic operations on integers, and will identify the properties that define a mathematical field, and apply those properties in the context of the field of Real Numbers.

  • 7.3
  • 7.13b
  • 7.16

Unit 2: Equations & Inequalities

This unit focuses on finding the solutions of linear equations and inequalities with one variable. Students will translate, and distinguish, between verbal and mathematical equations. Equations will be solved in at most two steps, and inequalities will be solved in at most one step. Students will set up and solve such equations to determine the solutions of real-world problems given in verbal form. Students will also graph solution sets to one-variable inequalities on a number line.

  • 7.13a
  • 7.14
  • 7.15

Unit 3: Functions

This unit explores relations between two variables and patterns of integers and rational numbers. Students will identify and derive functions of rational numbers using a variety of representations. They will also analyze and generalize sequences of integers and rational numbers.
