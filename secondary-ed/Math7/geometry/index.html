    Unit 5: Geometry

    Author
    Chris Harvey
    Updated
    Jan 31, 2014 23:55
    Mar 23, 2014 23:46
    Mar 26, 2014 22:09
    Apr 29, 2014 05:42

    This unit puts less emphasis on computation and arithmetic, and more emphasis on measurement and logical reasoning. Students will explore physical representations of number, including length, area, and volume, and explore the relationships between these attributes and how they are affected by one another. They will organize and categorize types of quadrilaterals based on properties involving sides, angles, and diagonals. Students will also represent and perform geometric transformations of polygons in the coordinate plane.

    Learning Objectives

    Perfect Squares No Calculator

    • Represent perfect squares and square roots geometrically.
      • Select or write an appropriate equation represented by a given diagram. Equations will be of the form \(a^2=A\) or \(\sqrt{A}=a\) (where \(a\) is an integer between 0 and 20).
      • Find the side length of a square with a given area.
      • Find the area of a square with a given side length.
    • Determine the square of an integer \(a\) between 0 and 20.
      • Expand \(a^2\) into factors.
      • Evaluate \(a^2\).
    • Determine the positive square root of a perfect square \(A\) between 0 and 400 (that is, \(A=a^2\) for some integer \(a\) between 0 and 20).
      • Evaluate \(\sqrt{A}\).
      • Evaluate \(\sqrt{\left(a^2\right)}\).
      • Evaluate \(\left(\sqrt{A}\right)^2\).

    Volume and Surface Area

    • Given rectangular prisms and cylinders may be represented as diagrams (a 3D projection onto a 2D surface) or as nets that are comprised of the faces of the solids.
    • Calculate the volume and surface area of a rectangular prism, given its length, width, and height. If the prism is a cube, only one measured attribute will be given.
    • Calculate the volume of a rectangular prism, given the area of its base and its height.
    • Find one measured attribute (either length, width, or height) of a rectangular prism given its volume and the other two attributes. If the prism is a cube, only the volume will be given.
    • Describe how the volume and surface area of a rectangular prism are affected by a change in one measured attribute (either length, width, or height). The attribute will be changed by a scale factor only.
    • Calculate the volume and surface area of a cylinder, given its radius and height.
    • Calculate the volume of a cylinder, given the area of its base and its height.
    • Determine whether a word problem calls for the application of volume or surface area.
    • Solve practical problems involving the volume and surface area of rectangular prisms and cylinders.

    Quadrilaterals

    • Identify and use geometric markings to represent congruent segments, parallel segments, congruent angles, and right angles in figures.
    • Identify properties of quadrilaterals, parallelograms, rectangles, rhombi, squares, trapezoids, and isosceles trapezoids. Properties will involve congruent/parallel (opposite) sides, congruent/right (opposite) angles, and bisecting/congruent/perpendicular diagonals.
    • Identify the most specific name of a given quadrilateral based on a description of its properties or a figure with geometric markings.
    • Identify categories to which a given quadrilateral belongs based on a description of its properties or a figure with geometric markings.

    Transformations

    • Given a figure (triangle or quadrilateral) in the coordinate plane, find the coordinates of the figure after performing either of the following transformations:
      • a translation involving at most two steps (one horizontal and/or one vertical)
      • a reflection over exactly one of the x- or y-axis
      • a dilation of 2, 3, 4, ½, or ¼ from the origin
      • a rotation of 90° or 180° clockwise or counter-clockwise about the origin
