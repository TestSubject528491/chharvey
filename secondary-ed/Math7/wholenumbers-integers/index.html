Unit 1: Whole Numbers & Integers

Updated Nov 18, 2013.

This unit focuses on performing, and identifying properties of, operations on whole numbers and integers. Students will simplify expressions involving the four basic operations on integers, and will identify the properties that define a mathematical field, and apply those properties in the context of the field of Real Numbers.

Learning Objectives

Operations on Integers

  • 7.3a Model addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of integers using manipulatives and pictorial representations.
    • Representations will include two-colored counters, blocks, and number lines.
    • Quotients of integer division problems will be integers as well.
  • 7.3b Compute the sum, difference, product, and quotient of two integers.
    • Determine whether a sum, difference, product, or quotient of two integers will be positive or negative, given those integers.
    • Determine whether a product or quotient of two integers will be positive or negative, given those integers' locations, relative to each other and relative to 0, on a number line.
    • Determine whether a sum or difference of two integers will be positive or negative, given those integers' locations, relative to each other and relative to 0, on a number line.
    • Complete word problems involving adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing integers.

Order of Operations

  • Compute the value of a numeric expression (one without variables) involving
    1. the four basic operations on whole numbers, and
    2. the same with parenthesis and exponents included (the exponent may be applied to a monomial or polynomial).
  • Identify the terms in a given numeric expression or an expression with variables, and classify such an expression as a monomial, binomial, trinomial, or polynomial.
  • Create a numeric expression equal to a target value.
  • 7.13b Evaluate an expression with at most three variables, given the replacement values of those variables.
    • Given an expression and the variables' replacement values, find the value of the expression.
    • Given the variables' replacement values and a target value, choose an expression, that when evaluated, is equal to the target value.
    • Given an expression and a target value, choose a set of variables' replacement vales that would make the expression equal to the target value. (This is identical to choosing values that satisfy an equation.)

Field Properties

  • 7.16 Identify and apply the following properties under the Real Numbers:
    1. commutative property of addition (CA)
    2. commutative property of multiplication (CM)
    3. associative property of addition (AA)
    4. associative property of multiplication (AM)
    5. identity property of addition (IA)
    6. identity property of multiplication (IM)
    7. inverse property of addition (NA)
    8. inverse property of multiplication (NM)
    9. zero property of multiplication (ZM)
    10. distributive property of multiplication over addition (DM)
    11. distributive property of exponents over multiplication (DE)
    • Given an equation, identify the property illustrated comparing the two sides
    • Given a step-by-step example of solving a two-step equation with one variable, identify properties used to justify steps in the equation-solving process.
