    Unit 2: Equations & Inequalities

    Updated Feb 19, 2014.

    This unit focuses on finding the solutions of linear equations and inequalities with one variable. Students will translate and distinguish between verbal and mathematical equations. Equations will be solved in at most two steps, and inequalities will be solved in at most one step. Students will set up and solve equations to determine the solutions of real-world problems given in verbal form. Students will also graph solution sets to one-variable inequalities on a number line.

    Learning Objectives

    Equations

    • Equations will be linear and in one variable.
    • Model equations with blocks or pictorial diagrams.
    • Solve one-variable linear equations of the following forms, where \(a\), \(b\), \(c\), \(p\), and \(q\) are integers or terminating decimals:
      1. in one step:
        • \(x+b=c\)
        • \(ax=c\)
        • \(\frac{x}{q}=c\)
        • \(\frac{p}{q}x=c\)
      2. in two steps:
        • \(ax+b=c\)
        • \(\frac{x}{q}+b=c\)
        • \(\frac{p}{q}x+b=c\)
        • \(p\left(x+b\right)=c\)
        • \(\frac{x+b}{q}=c\)
      3. in at most two steps by combining like terms:
        • \(ax+b=cx+d\)
    • Identify properties used to justify steps in solving an equation.
      • Properties of operations (field properties)
      • Properties of equality
        • additive property of equality
        • subtractive property of equality
        • multiplicative property of equality
        • division property of equality

    Inequalities

    • Inequalities will be linear and in one variable.
    • Identify possible solutions to:
      • a given inequality with the variable isolated
      • a given inequality with the variable not isolated
      • the graph of an inequality
    • Graph the solution set of an inequality on a number line.
    • Model inequalities with blocks or pictorial diagrams.
    • Solve inequalities in one step.
      1. Solve an inequality using addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.
      2. Solve an inequality involving a variable with a negative coefficient.

    Verbal Mathematics

    • Translate between verbals and mathematics.
      • Translate verbal phrases to and from mathematical expressions, containing at most two operations.
      • Translate verbal sentences to and from mathematical equations, containing at most two operations.
      • Translate verbal sentences to and from mathematical inequalities, containing at most one operation.
    • Identify differences between mathematical expressions and mathematical equations.
    • Solve verbal sentences in at most two steps, using mathematical equations.
    • Solve verbal sentences in at most one step, using mathematical inequalities.
    • Solve practical problems that involving equations.
      • Given a word problem, select or construct an appropriate equation that models the real-world situation.
      • Given a word problem, find the solution in at most two steps.
      • Equations to word problems will involve only the following forms, where \(a\), \(b\), \(c\), and \(q\) are integers or terminating decimals:
        • \(ax+b=c\)
        • \(\frac{x+b}{q}=c\)

    Vocabulary

    equation
    a statement that compares two expressions, indicating that they have the same value
    inequality
    a statement that compares two expressions, indicating that one has a greater or lesser value than the other
    solution
    a value, that when substituted in for the variable, makes a statement true
    solution set
    the set of all solutions for a statement
    solve
    to find the solution of a statement
    isolate the variable
    to get the variable by itself on one side of the equals symbol or inequality symbol
    inverse operation
    an operation, that when performed, “un-does” another operation. addition and subtraction are inverse operations, and multiplication and division are inverse operations
