Unit 4: Rational Numbers & Proportions

Updated Jan 9, 2014.

In this unit, students' learning will center on proportional reasoning, a subject that is emphasized throughout the curriculum. Students will explore different representations of rational numbers and their placement with respect to each other. They will also investigate the concepts of ratio and proportion, and use these to solve practical problems involving real-world scenarios, including geometric polygons and prisms.

Learning Objectives

Distance

Perfect Squares

  • Determine square roots of perfect squares between 0 and 400.
  • Determine the square of an integer between 0 and 20.
  • Represent square roots and perfect squares geometrically.

Absolute Value

  • Demonstrate absolute value using a number line.
  • Determine the absolute value of a rational number.
  • Represent the distance between two rational numbers using absolute value.

Scientific Notation

  • Convert among decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.
  • Compare decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.
  • Order decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.
  1. Compare (determine which is greater) decimals to decimals; percents to percents; and decimals to percents.
  2. Compare fractions to fractions.
  3. Represent integral powers of ten as fractions and decimals. Exponents will be between -10 and 10.
  4. Convert between scientific notation and decimals.
  5. Convert between scientific notation and fractions.
  6. Convert between fractions and decimals.
  7. Order decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.

Proportions

  • Solve practical problems using proportional reasoning.
  • Determine whether two polygons are similar.
  • Write proportions to express relationships between corresponding sides of similar polygons.
  • Polygons will be limited to triangles and quadrilaterals.
  • Calculate the volume and surface area of rectangular prisms and cylinders.
  • Solve practical problems involving the volume and surface area of rectangular prisms and cylinders.
  • Determine whether a problem calls for the application of volume or surface area.
  • Describe how the volume and surface area of a rectangular prism are affected by a change in one measured attribute (either length, width, or height). The attribute will be changed by a scale factor only.
