Unit 3: Rational Numbers & Proportions

Updated Feb 11, 2014.

In this unit, students' learning will center on proportional reasoning. Students will explore different representations of positive rational numbers, familiarize their placement with respect to each other in a total order, and make connections between magnitude and order. They will also investigate the concepts of ratio and proportion, and use these to solve practical problems involving real-world scenarios.

Learning Objectives

Rational Numbers No Calculator

  1. Convert integral powers of ten among multiple forms of representation. Exponents will be integers between -12 and 12.
    • Convert a positive power of ten among the following:
      exponent form (\(10^n\), where \(n\) is an integer between 1 and 12)
      \[10^3\]
      expanded form (repeated multiplication)
      \[10 \cdot 10 \cdot 10\]
      standard decimal form
      \[1000\]
    • Convert a negative power of ten among the following:
      exponent form (\(10^n\), where \(n\) is an integer between -1 and -12)
      \[10^{-3}\]
      standard decimal form
      \[0.001\]
      reduced fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{1000}\]
      expanded fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{10 \cdot 10 \cdot 10}\]
      exponential fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{10^3}\]
      expansion of fraction form (repeated multiplication)
      \[\frac{1}{10} \cdot \frac{1}{10} \cdot \frac{1}{10}\]
      exponent of a fraction form
      \(\left(\frac{1}{10}\right)^3\)
  2. Convert between scientific notation and decimals. Scientific notation will be in the form \(c \times 10^n\), where \(c\) is a rational number \(1 \le c \lt 10\), whose decimal is limited to the thousandths place, and \(n\) is an integer \(-12 \le n \le 12\).
  3. Compare determine which is greater: decimals, percents, and scientific notation (among all three forms).
  4. Compare fractions to fractions. Denominators will be integers between 1 and 12.
  5. Convert between fractions and decimals.
  6. Compare decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation (among all four forms).
  7. Order decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation, given four rational numbers in any form.

Absolute Value

  • Describe the absolute value of a rational number as the distance between that number and zero on the number line.
  • Evaluate the absolute value of a rational number.
  • Identify true and false equations containing absolute values. Equations will be of the form \(\left|p\right|=q\), \(p=\left|q\right|\), or \(\left|p\right|=\left|q\right|\), where \(p\) and \(q\) are (positive or negative) rational numbers.
  • Represent the distance between two rational numbers using absolute value. If \(p\) and \(q\) are rational numbers, then the distance between \(p\) and \(q\) is calculated by \(\left|p-q\right|\). Note that \(\left|p-q\right|=\left|q-p\right|\).

Proportions

  • Solve practical problems using proportional reasoning.
  • Determine whether two polygons are similar (polygons will be limited to triangles and quadrilaterals).
  • Write proportions to express relationships between corresponding sides of similar polygons.
