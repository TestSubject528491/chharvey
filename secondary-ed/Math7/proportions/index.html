Unit 3: Rational Numbers & Proportions

In this unit, students' learning will center on proportional reasoning. Students will explore different representations of positive rational numbers, familiarize their placement with respect to each other in a total order, and make connections between magnitude and order. They will also investigate the concepts of ratio and proportion, and use these to solve practical problems involving real-world scenarios.

Learning Objectives

Rational Numbers No Calculator

  1. Convert between decimals, percents, and fractions.
  2. Convert integral powers of ten among multiple forms of representation. Exponents will be integers between -12 and 12.
    • Convert a positive power of ten (\(10^n\), where \(n\) is an integer between 1 and 12) among the following forms: exponent form, expanded form (iterated multiplication), and standard form.
      exponent form
      \[10^3\]
      expanded form
      \[10 \times 10 \times 10\]
      standard form
      \[1000\]
    • Investigate the identity \(10^0=1\) through the use of patterns.

    • Convert a negative power of ten (\(10^n\), where \(n\) is an integer between -1 and -12) among the following forms: exponent form, exponent denominator form, expanded denominator form1, simplest fraction form, standard form. These forms are best taught in the order mentioned.

      exponent form
      \[10^{-3}\]
      exponent denominator form
      \[\frac{1}{10^3}\]
      expanded denominator form
      \[\frac{1}{10 \times 10 \times 10}\]
      simplest fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{1000}\]
      standard form
      \[0.001\]

      Students will also explore the following forms: expansion of a fraction form, expansion of a decimal form (both iterated multiplication), exponent of a fraction form, and exponent of a decimal form.

      expansion of fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{10} \times \frac{1}{10} \times \frac{1}{10}\]
      expansion of decimal form
      \[0.1 \times 0.1 \times 0.1\]
      exponent of a fraction form
      \[\left(\frac{1}{10}\right)^3\]
      exponent of a decimal form
      \[\left(0.1\right)^3\]
  3. Convert between scientific notation and decimals. Scientific notation will be in the form \(c \times 10^n\), where \(c\) is a decimal rational number \(1 \le c \lt 10\) limited to the thousandths place, and \(n\) is an integer \(-12 \le n \le 12\).
  4. Compare decimals, percents, and scientific notation (among all three forms).
  5. Compare fractions to fractions, without converting to decimals. Denominators will be integers between 2 and 12.
    • Compare fractions with the same numerator.
    • Compare fractions with the same denominator.
    • Compare fractions with different numerators and denominators.
  6. Compare fractions to fractions by converting to decimals. Denominators will be integers between 2 and 12.
  7. Order decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation, given four rational numbers in any form.
  8. Order rational numbers by placing them on a number line.

Absolute Value

  • Describe the absolute value of a rational number as the distance between that number and zero on the number line.
  • Evaluate the absolute value of a rational number.
  • Identify true and false equations containing absolute values. Equations will be of the form \(\left|p\right|=q\), \(p=\left|q\right|\), or \(\left|p\right|=\left|q\right|\), where \(p\) and \(q\) are (positive or negative) rational numbers.
  • Represent the distance between two rational numbers using absolute value. If \(p\) and \(q\) are rational numbers, then the distance between \(p\) and \(q\) is calculated by \(\left|p-q\right|\). Note that \(\left|p-q\right|=\left|q-p\right|\).

Tax, Tips, and Discounts

  • Given the original price of a purchase and the tax, tip, or discount rate, find
    • either the amount paid for tax or tip, or the amount saved from the discount, and
    • the total cost of the purchase.
  • Given an original price and the total cost, find the tax/tip/discount rate for the sale. If \(o\) represents the original price and \(t\) represents the total cost, then the rate is given by \((t-o)/o\) if it is a tax/tip, but \((o-t)/o\) if it is a discount.
  • Given the tax/tip/discount rate of a purchase and the total cost, find the original price. If \(r\) represents the rate and \(t\) represents the total cost, then the original price is given by \(t/\left(1+r\right)\) if \(r\) is a tax/tip, but \(t/\left(1-r\right)\) if \(r\) is a discount.
  • Solve a multistep problem in which a discount, a tax rate, and a tip rate are applied, in that order: Given the original price and all three rates, find the total cost. Problems such as this may be reduced to only two percent computations.

Ratios, Rates, and Proportions

  • Distinguish between ratios, rates, and proportions.
  • Determine the ratio of two given quantities. Given quantities may form part-to-part or part-to-total relationships.
  • Compare two ratios (using the Compare Fractions to Fractions objective above).
  • Determine the unit rate of a given rate, and compare two unit rates.
  • Determine if two ratios form a proportion. Two ratios/rates form a proportion if and only if they are equivalent (equal).
  • Solve a proportion of the form \(\frac{a}{b}=\frac{x}{c}\) or \(\frac{a}{b}=\frac{c}{x}\), where \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are rational, and \(x\) is unknown.
  • Write and solve a proportion that corresponds to a given word problem.
  • Solve practical problems involving converting betewen the U.S. Customary and Metric systems.
  • Solve practical problems involving scale drawings of maps and floor plans.

Similar Polygons

  • Write proportions to express relationships between corresponding sides of similar polygons.
  • Determine whether two polygons are similar. Polygons will be limited to triangles and quadrilaterals, and at least two sides of each polygon will be given.
  • Given two similar polygons, find a missing side length. One polygon will have at least two sides given and the other will have at least one side given.
  • Solve practical problems involving similar triangles and rectangles.

Vocabulary

friendly fraction
a fraction whose denominator is a factor of 100 (1, 2, 5, 10, 20, 50, 100)
perentage
a fraction whose denominator is 100
power
a base raised to an exponent, for example, “the third power of 10” is \(10^3\)
coefficient
a number being multiplied
scientific notation
a shorter way to write very large and very small numbers
“small” number
a number between 0 and 1
“medium” number
a number between 1 and 10
“large” number
a number greater than 10
less than
to the left of, on a number line
greater than
to the right of, on a number line
tax rate
the amount added to the price by law
tip rate
the amount added to the price by the customer
discount rate
the amount removed from the price by the seller
ratio
a multiplicative comparison of two quantities
part-to-part
a ratio comparing two parts of a total group
part-to-total
a ratio comparing part of a group to the total group
rate
a ratio comparing two quantities with different units
unit rate
a rate in which the denominator is reduced to 1 unit
proportion
an equation stating two ratios or two rates are equal
scale factor
congruent
similar
corresponding angle
corresponding side
