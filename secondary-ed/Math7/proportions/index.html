Unit 3: Rational Numbers & Proportions

Updated Feb 26, 2014.

In this unit, students' learning will center on proportional reasoning. Students will explore different representations of positive rational numbers, familiarize their placement with respect to each other in a total order, and make connections between magnitude and order. They will also investigate the concepts of ratio and proportion, and use these to solve practical problems involving real-world scenarios.

Learning Objectives

Rational Numbers No Calculator

  1. Convert integral powers of ten among multiple forms of representation. Exponents will be integers between -12 and 12.
    • Convert a positive power of ten (\(10^n\), where \(n\) is an integer between 1 and 12) among the following:
      exponent form
      \[10^3\]
      expanded form (repeated multiplication)
      \[10 \cdot 10 \cdot 10\]
      standard form
      \[1000\]
    • Convert a negative power of ten (\(10^n\), where \(n\) is an integer between -1 and -12) among the following:
      exponent form
      \[10^{-3}\]
      standard form
      \[0.001\]
      simplest fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{1000}\]
      expanded fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{10 \cdot 10 \cdot 10}\]
      exponential fraction form
      \[\frac{1}{10^3}\]
      expansion of fraction form (repeated multiplication)
      \[\frac{1}{10} \cdot \frac{1}{10} \cdot \frac{1}{10}\]
      exponent of a fraction form
      \[\left(\frac{1}{10}\right)^3\]
    • Investigate the identity \(10^0=1\) through the use of patterns.
  2. Convert between scientific notation and decimals. Scientific notation will be in the form \(c \times 10^n\), where \(c\) is a rational number \(1 \le c \lt 10\), whose decimal is limited to the thousandths place, and \(n\) is an integer \(-12 \le n \le 12\).
  3. Convert between decimals, percents, and fractions.
  4. Compare decimals, percents, and scientific notation (among all three forms).
  5. Compare fractions to fractions, without converting to decimals. Denominators will be integers between 2 and 12.
    • Compare fractions with the same numerator.
    • Compare fractions with the same denominator.
    • Compare fractions with different numerators and denominators.
  6. Compare fractions to fractions by converting to decimals. Denominators will be integers between 2 and 12.
  7. Order decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation, given four rational numbers in any form.

Absolute Value

  • Describe the absolute value of a rational number as the distance between that number and zero on the number line.
  • Evaluate the absolute value of a rational number.
  • Identify true and false equations containing absolute values. Equations will be of the form \(\left|p\right|=q\), \(p=\left|q\right|\), or \(\left|p\right|=\left|q\right|\), where \(p\) and \(q\) are (positive or negative) rational numbers.
  • Represent the distance between two rational numbers using absolute value. If \(p\) and \(q\) are rational numbers, then the distance between \(p\) and \(q\) is calculated by \(\left|p-q\right|\). Note that \(\left|p-q\right|=\left|q-p\right|\).

Tax, Tips, and Discounts

  • Given the original price of a purchase and the tax, tip, or discount rate, find
    • either the amount paid for tax or tip, or the amount saved from the discount, and
    • the total cost of the purchase.
  • Given an original price and the total cost, find the tax/tip/discount rate for the sale.
  • Given the tax/tip/discount rate of a purchase and the total cost, find the original price. If \(t\) represents the total cost and \(r\) represents the rate, then the original price is given by \(t/\left(1+r\right)\) if \(r\) is tax/tip, but \(t/\left(1-r\right)\) if \(r\) is a discount.
  • Solve a multistep problem in which a discount, a tax rate, and a tip rate are applied, in that order.

Ratios, Rates, and Proportions

  • Distinguish between ratios, rates, and proportions.
  • Determine the unit rate of a given rate.
  • Compare (decide which is greater) two ratios or rates, and determine if they are equivalent. Two ratios/rates are equivalent (equal) if and only if they form a proportion.
  • Solve a proportion of the form \(\frac{a}{b}=\frac{x}{c}\) or \(\frac{a}{b}=\frac{c}{x}\), where \(x\) is unknown.
  • Write and solve a proportion that corresponds to a given word problem.
  • Use proportional reasoning to solve practical problems involving converting units betewen the U.S. Customary and Metric systems.

Scale Drawings

  • Use proportional reasoning to solve practical problems involving scale drawings (of maps, floorplans, etc.).
  • Determine whether two polygons are similar. Polygons will be limited to triangles and quadrilaterals.
  • Write proportions to express relationships between corresponding sides of similar polygons.
