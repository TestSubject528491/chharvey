Unit 4: Rational Numbers & Proportions

Updated Jan 9, 2014.

In this unit, students' learning will center on proportional reasoning. Students will explore different representations of positive rational numbers, familiarize their placement with respect to each other in a total order, and make connections between magnitude and order. They will also investigate the concepts of ratio and proportion, and use these to solve practical problems involving real-world scenarios, including two- and three-dimensional geometric figures.

Learning Objectives

Perfect Squares

  • Represent perfect squares and square roots geometrically.
    • Select or write an appropriate equation represented by a given diagram. Equations will be of the form \(a^2=A\) or \(\sqrt{A}=a\) (where \(a\) is an integer between 0 and 20).
    • Find the side length of a square with a given area.
    • Find the area of a square with a given side length.
  • Determine the square of an integer \(a\) between 0 and 20.
    • Expand \(a^2\) into factors.
    • Evaluate \(a^2\).
  • Determine the positive square root of a perfect square \(A\) between 0 and 400 (that is, \(A=a^2\) for some integer \(a\) between 0 and 20).
    • Evaluate \(\sqrt{A}\).
    • Evaluate \(\sqrt{\left(a^2\right)}\).
    • Evaluate \(\left(\sqrt{A}\right)^2\).

Absolute Value

  • Describe the absolute value of a rational number as the distance between that number and zero on the number line.
  • Evaluate the absolute value of a rational number.
  • Identify true and false equations containing absolute values. Equations will be of the form \(\left|p\right|=q\) or \(p=\left|q\right|\), where \(p\) and \(q\) are (positive or negative) rational numbers.
  • Represent the distance between two rational numbers using absolute value. If \(p\) and \(q\) are rational numbers, then the distance between \(p\) and \(q\) is calculated by \(\left|p-q\right|\). Note that \(\left|p-q\right|=\left|q-p\right|\).

Scientific Notation

  • Convert among decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.
  • Compare decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.
  • Order decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.
  1. Compare (determine which is greater) decimals to decimals; percents to percents; and decimals to percents.
  2. Compare fractions to fractions.
  3. Represent powers of ten as fractions and decimals.
    • Exponents will be integers between -12 and 12.
    • Fractions will be in simplest form, or in the form of \(\frac{1}{10^a}\), where \(a\) is an integer.
    • Decimals will be limited to the thousandths place.
  4. Convert between scientific notation and decimals.
  5. Convert between scientific notation and fractions.
  6. Convert between fractions and decimals.
  7. Order decimals, percents, fractions, and scientific notation.

Proportions

  • Solve practical problems using proportional reasoning.
  • Determine whether two polygons are similar.
  • Write proportions to express relationships between corresponding sides of similar polygons.
  • Polygons will be limited to triangles and quadrilaterals.
  • Calculate the volume and surface area of a given rectangular prism and cylinder. A rectangular prism and a cylinder may be represented as a diagram (a 3D projection onto a 2D surface) or as a net that is comprised of the faces of the solid.
  • Solve practical problems involving the volume and surface area of rectangular prisms and cylinders.
  • Determine whether a problem calls for the application of volume or surface area.
  • Describe how the volume and surface area of a rectangular prism are affected by a change in one measured attribute (either length, width, or height). The attribute will be changed by a scale factor only.
