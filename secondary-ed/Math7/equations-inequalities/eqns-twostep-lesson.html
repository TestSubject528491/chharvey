Lesson 2.3: Two-Step Equations

SOL
7.14a The student will solve one- and two-step linear equations in one variable
7.14b The student will solve practical problems involving the solution of one- and two-step linear equations
Timeframe
Week II.1
Time required
7 days
Lesson created
Oct 10, 2013
Lesson updated
Nov 5, 2013

Preparation

Objectives

  • Solve a linear equation in two steps.
  • Solve word problems using the equations.

Essential Questions

  • What is a linear equation?
  • How do I know which step to perform first?
  • What properties do I use to solve equations?

Vocabulary

equation
a statement that says that two expressions have the same value
isolate
to get the variable by itself on one side of the equals symbol (\(=\))
inverse operation
an operation, that when performed, un-does another operation
addition and subtraction are inverse operations, and multiplication and division are inverse operations
solve
to find the value of the variable

Lesson Implementation

Warm-up

students will complete Pre-Quiz 2.2

Instruction, Part A

Day 1

solve equations of the form \(ax+b=c\) using blocks

Give students a Sentences Mat and blocks to work with. Show example problems from the Two-Step Equations worksheet from the Academy Handbook on the board. Model example problems and ask students to use the blocks to complete the other problems.

After modeling with blocks, show more examples on board with drawing. Have students copy with paper and pencil to practice drawing the manipulatives.

Day 2

solve equations of the form \(ax+b=c\) using written steps

Give students a copy of the Two-Step Equations worksheet from the Academy Handbook. For the first problem, draw the manipulatives on the board but tell students not to copy. Fill in the Steps on the worksheet after each step is performed with the blocks, students copy the steps. After the steps are done, show them how to write the steps for solving (e.g. "-3 -3", "/4 /4").

Day 3

solve equations of the forms \(\frac{x}{q}+b=c\) and \(\frac{p}{q}x+b=c\) using written steps

Part 1: Practice problems of the form \(\frac{x}{q}+b=c\) using written steps, like Day 2. The only difference is, the variable is being divided.

Solving Two-Step Equations with Properties worksheet

Part 2: Practice with one-step equations of the form \(\frac{p}{q}x=c\) first. Use the Inverse Property of Multiplication to "get rid" of the fraction. Do not teach students to turn it into a decimal.

Day 4

solve equations of the forms \(p\left(x+b\right)=c\) and \(\frac{x+b}{q}=c\) using written steps

Instruction, Part B

Days 5–7

solve word problems involving two-step equations
