Unit 3: Functions

Updated Nov 20, 2013.

This unit explores relations between two variables and patterns of integers and rational numbers. Students will identify and derive functions of rational numbers using a variety of representations. They will also analyze and generalize sequences of integers and rational numbers.

Learning Objectives

Relations

  • 7.13a Translate between verbals and mathematics
    • Translate verbal phrases to and from mathematical expressions, containing at most two operations.
    • Translate verbal sentences to and from mathematical equations.
    • Translate verbal sentences to and from mathematical inequalities.
    • Identify differences between mathematical expressions and mathematical statements.

Sequences

  • Model one-variable linear equations with blocks. Equations will be solved in at most two steps.
  • 7.14a Solve one-variable linear equations of the following forms, where \(a\), \(b\), \(c\), \(p\), and \(q\) are integers:
    1. in one step:
      • \(x+b=c\)
      • \(ax=c\)
      • \(\frac{x}{q}=c\)
      • \(\frac{p}{q}x=c\)
    2. in two steps:
      • \(ax+b=c\)
      • \(\frac{x}{q}+b=c\)
      • \(\frac{p}{q}x+b=c\)
      • \(p\left(x+b\right)=c\)
      • \(\frac{x+b}{q}=c\)
    3. in at most two steps and by combining like terms:
      • \(ax+b=cx+d\)
  • 7.14b Solve practical problems requiring the solution of one-variable linear equations.
    • Given a word problem, students will select or construct an appropriate equation that models the real-world situation.
    • Given a word problem, students will find the solution in at most two steps.

Inequalities

  • 7.15b Graph the solution set of one-variable linear inequalities on a number line.
    • Given an inequality with the variable isolated, graph the solution set on a number line.
    • Given the graph of the solution set to an inequality, write the inequality.
  • 7.15a Solve one-variable linear inequalities in one step.
    1. Solve an inequality using addition or subtraction
    2. Solve an inequality using multiplication or division
    3. Solve an inequality with a negative coefficient

Vocabulary

equation
a statement that compares two expressions, indicating that they have the same value
inequality
a statement that compares two expressions, indicating that one has a greater or lesser value than the other
solution
a value, that when substituted in for the variable, makes a statement true
solution set
the set of all solutions for a statement
solve
to find the solution of a statement
isolate the variable
to get the variable by itself on one side of the equals symbol or inequality symbol
inverse operation
an operation, that when performed, un-does another operation
addition and subtraction are inverse operations, and multiplication and division are inverse operations
