Classical Music Cataloging System

A Proposed Cataloging System for Historical Art Music (Classical Music)

The application iTunes™ by Apple is a great system for storing your music. For classical music aficionados, however, there is little to no support for classical music, more accurately known as historical art music. (The term Classical Music technically applies to historical art music composed in the Classical period: 1750–1825.)

This proposed system works cleanly with iTunes™ and requires no updates or third-party support. With this system, you will be able to store, browse, and find your music efficiently and quickly. Even on your iPod™ you’ll be able to know what piece is playing at a glance.

Fields

The following fields of the track will be necessarily used:

  1. Title
  2. Artist
  3. Album Artist
  4. Composer
  5. Genre
  6. Grouping

If the contents of a field are unavailable, leave the field blank.

I will discuss each field in the preceding order, except Title will be lastly outlined as it is the most complex.

Artist

The Artist field shall contain the solo performer of the work, or the concertmaster, or the conductor of the orchestra. The format of the Artist field shall take the following form.

First Middle Last

Album Artist

The Album Artist field shall contain the proper name of the performing orchestra or ensemble. If the Album Artist is an individual, it shall be equal to the Artist.

Composer

The Composer field shall contain the first and last names (and optionally, the middle name) and life dates of the writer of the work.

Last, First Middle (YOB-YOD) YOB = Year Of Birth; YOD = Year Of Death. Use a normal hyphen between these, rather than a proper en dash.

Genre

The Genre field shall contain the instrumentation for which the piece was composed. Some examples include symphony, concerto, and sonata. The feature instrument shall not be included. The format of the Genre shall be the genre as a proper noun.

Genre

Grouping

The Grouping field shall contain the era to which the composer belonged, or during which the piece was written. It shall be one of the following strings:

  • Ancient
  • Early
  • Renaissance
  • Classical
  • Romantic
  • Modern

Title

The form of the Title field is described in this section. The labels shall be recorded in the order in which they are listed below, without any characters between them. If information is unavailable, the label shall be left blank.

  1. Composer
  2. Supergenre
  3. Genre
  4. Subgenre
  5. Instrument
  6. Work Number
  7. Movement Number
  8. Key
  9. Mode
  10. Index
  11. Year of Composition
  12. Title of Work
  13. Title of Movement
  14. Tempo Marking(s)

When put together, a track title with all of the above components might look like the following.

Original name:
Variations on Piano Concerto No. 2 in C-Sharp Minor, Op. 17a No. 1 "Apollo" III. Scherzo: Largo; Presto
by Harvey, Christopher Hewitt (1987–) in 2009
New name:
Har.CH&CTO$VAR.PO.2.III@C#-m,Op17aNo1_2009 "Apollo" Scherzo: Largo; Presto
A working example:
Bee.LV&SFY.5.I@C-m, Op67_1808 Allegro con brio

Composer

The first three letters of the composer’s last name, with the first letter majuscule and the last two miniscule, shall be recorded, followed by a period mark, and then the majuscule initial letter of the composer’s first name (and that of the middle name if this is by which the composer is known better).

  • Bac.JS
  • Moz.WA
  • Bee.LV
  • Cho.F
  • Deb.C
  • Sho.D

Supergenre

If a supergenre exists, the label shall be recorded exactly like the genre label except followed by a percent sign (%) rather than an ampersand mark (&).

Genre

The genre label shall succeed an ampersand mark and shall be equivalent to the Genre field in the song info, represented with three majuscule characters. If the code is not listed, the genre shall be written out with the first letter majuscule and the others miniscule.

symphony
&SFY
concerto
&CTO
sonata
&STA
duet, trio, quartet, quintet
&D2T, &T3O, &Q4T, & Q5T
character piece
&CPC
suite
&SWT
prelude and fugue
&PNF
invention, sinfonia
&IVN, &SFA
opera or oratorio
&ORA
fantasy, fantasia, or caprice
&FSE

Subgenre

The subgenre label shall be recorded exactly like the genre label except followed by a dollar sign ($) rather than an ampersand mark (&). If the code is not listed, the subgenre shall be written out with the first letter majuscule and the others miniscule.

prelude, intermezzo, postlude
$PRE, $INT, $PST
nocturne, scherzo
$NCT, $SZO
etude, impromptu
$ETD, $IPT
ballad, waltz
$BLD, $WLZ
polonaise, mazurka
$POL, $MZA
song without words
$SWW
variations
$VAR

Instrument

The instrument label shall represent the solo for which the work was originally written by two majuscule characters, and shall succeed a period mark. If no instrument is featured, leave blank.

flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon, saxophone
.FL .OB .CL .BN .SX
horn, trumpet, trombone, tuba
.HN .TP .TB .TA
violin, viola, cello, bass
.VN .VA .CO .CB
woodwinds, brass, strings, choir/chorus
.WW .BR .ST .CH
piano, voice
.PO .VO

Work number

The work number label shall represent the original No. dubbed to the work. It shall be recorded as an Arabic numeral succeeding a period mark.

  • .2
  • .9

Movement number

The movement number shall be recorded as either an Arabic or Roman numeral; whichever is traditionally indexed. The numeral shall succeed a period mark.

  • .3
  • .II

Key

The key of the track shall be recorded as the at symbol (@) preceding a single majuscule letter, succeeded by a pound symbol (#) (for sharp) or minuscule letter b (for flat) if necessary.

  • @C
  • @F#
  • @Eb

Mode

The mode of the track (major or minor) shall be recorded as a hyphen succeeded by a majuscule or minuscule letter m; whichever is necessary.

  • -M
  • -m

Index

The index number of the piece shall be recorded as traditionally, except without punctuation. The number is preceded with a comma mark.

  • ,BWV850
  • ,HobXVI23
  • ,K332
  • ,Op101
  • ,Op28No9

Year of composition

The year of completion of composition shall be recorded succeeding an underscore. If composition year is unknown, use publication year or any other approximation. Otherwise leave blank.

  • _1776
  • _1802

Title of work

Some works are dubbed a title. This shall be recorded exactly as traditionally indexed, surrounded with double-quotation marks (not left- or right- quotes). The title in quotes shall be both preceded and succeeded by a space.

  • "Emperor"
  • "Eroica"
  • "Nutcracker"

Title of movement

The title of the movement, if it exists, shall be recorded exactly as traditionally indexed, succeeded by a colon mark and a non-breaking space, only if tempo markings are available. Otherwise the colon mark and space shall be omitted.

  • Intermezzo: 
  • Finale: 
  • Rondo

Tempo marking(s)

Tempo markings, if available, shall be recorded exactly as traditionally indexed, with only the first letter of the marking majuscule and separate tempo markings separated with a semicolon and non-breaking space.

  • Allegro con brio
  • Allegro moderato; Prestissimo

Conclusions About Functionality

With this new system, the Name field in iTunes (or other music library programs) is shortened greatly, thus making it easier for the listener to see what he or she is listening to when checking the digital music player. The codes are set logically and systematically, avoiding any ambiguity to the listener, while shortening the length of the track name. Yet the system retains the titles of the work and movements in their original form so the listener can instantly recognize the composer and piece. In addition, the important aspects of the piece such as opus number and movement number are moved toward the front of the text field so that it will show on digital music players that do not scroll or marquee text.
