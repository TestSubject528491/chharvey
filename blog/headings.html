What kind of heading is it?
The semantics and style of nested headings in Webkit

Although the HTML5 spec does not state that the body Element is a sectioning content Element, it really is. I have learned from experience not to use the article section (because it is sectioning content) as the only direct descendent (a.k.a. child) of the body Element. It creates an unnecessary subsection. The article Element is meant for use when multiple sub-sections (namely, articles) of the body are present.

Conclusion

Based on explicit and implicit sections, Webkit browsers will style h1 Elements “properly”. For example, an h1 in a section in the body semantically acts as a level-2 heading. Therefore the Webkit browser will style it as if it were an h2.

However, all other headings, h2–h6, have constant styles. That is, an h2, no matter want it means (semantically, based on explicit/implicit sectioning), will always appear as an h2.
